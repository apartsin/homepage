Research Focus

Home / Research / Research Focus

My research focuses on applied AI for robust perception across vision, voice, signal, and text; spec-driven generative systems; and distributed agentic intelligence.

Robust AI for Vision / Language / Signals

Perception and inference that hold up under adverse conditions across all modalities: visual degradation and extreme geometry, speech and text under noise and ambiguity, anomaly detection in sensor and signal data. Recent work: license plate recoverability under extreme angles, critical information extraction from distress communications, and implicit entity recognition in narratives.

Spec-Driven GenAI

Controllable generation of molecules, text, and images under target constraints — where the specification drives the output rather than free-form prompting. The core challenge is steering generation toward measurable targets without collapsing diversity or ignoring domain structure. Active projects: domain-gated latent diffusion for energetic materials, interactive diffusion steering, and speculative code generation.

Distributed AI

Coordination, evaluation, and reasoning across multiple agents under limited information — no shared state, no direct communication, no global priors. The unifying question is how meaningful structure emerges when agents can only observe outcomes and act independently. Active projects: zero-knowledge swarm coordination, self-evaluating model ensembles, and collaborative reasoning framework.
