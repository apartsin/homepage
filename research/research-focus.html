Research Focus

Home / Research / Research Focus

My research focuses on applied AI for robust perception, reasoning, and decision making under real-world conditions, validated through peer-reviewed publications and open-source projects.

Robust Perception & Reasoning

Applied AI systems that maintain reliable performance under real-world conditions: noisy inputs, incomplete data, distribution shifts, and adversarial edge cases across language, vision, and multimodal sensory data. Recent work includes chest X-ray classification with rejection mechanisms and early-exit optimization for CNN inference.

Generative AI for Evaluation

Using generative models to create reliable training, testing, and evaluation settings when real data is limited, skewed toward ideal cases, or insufficient to cover the distribution of operational scenarios. This includes work on evaluating synthetic vs. real data for code review and benchmarking diagnostic questioning efficiency of LLMs.

Agentic & Distributed Intelligence

Systems that combine active information gathering, iterative refinement, and hybrid reasoning to support decision making in complex environments. This spans multi-agent coordination, tool-using agents, and architectures that integrate perception with planning, as in panoramic incident summarization from body-worn footage and explainable semantic text relation frameworks.

Dependable AI Systems

Bridging the gap between laboratory accuracy and operational reliability. AI systems that are not only precise under controlled conditions, but dependable in realistic deployment settings, as explored in LLM-guided headline rewriting without clickbait and resume seniority classification with LLMs.
