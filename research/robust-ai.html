Robust AI for Vision, Language, and Signals

Home / Research / Robust AI

The pragmatic test for perception is not benchmark accuracy but operational reliability: does the system still work when inputs are degraded, geometry is extreme, or language is noisy and ambiguous? This direction studies inference across vision, language, and signals as they actually arrive in the field.

Overview

Most AI benchmarks are built on clean data. Robust AI asks a harder question: what happens when the data isn't clean? This direction covers three modalities united by the same concern. In vision, it means classification and detection under degraded resolution, extreme viewing angles, and domain shift. In language, it means extraction and understanding when text is noisy, telegraphic, or missing key mentions. In signals, it means making sense of non-standard inputs such as interjections, fuzzy speech, and behavioral traces in query logs.

A recurring theme is active information seeking: rather than waiting for clean inputs, robust systems must infer, reconstruct, and reason from whatever is available.

Robust Vision

Image classification with early-exit cascades, license plate recoverability under extreme angles, multi-pathology chest X-ray classification with rejection, and panoramic incident reconstruction from multi-camera footage.

Robust Language

Critical information extraction from distress communications, implicit entity recognition without explicit mentions, epistemic robustness benchmarking of LLMs, and understanding under noise, ambiguity, and domain shift.

Robust Signals

Interjection and non-verbal signal classification, medical insight from fuzzy speech, and health crisis detection from behavioral signals in online medication queries.

Active Projects

CalexNet CNN early-exit cascades closing train-inference mismatches for robust, efficient image classification. ImplicitEntities Benchmark for recognizing named entities from contextual cues alone, without explicit mentions. EMR-ACH LLM geopolitical forecasting as structured evidence matrix using Analysis of Competing Hypotheses.
