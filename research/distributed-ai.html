Distributed and Agentic AI

Home / Research / Distributed and Agentic AI

The pragmatic test for multi-agent AI is not protocol elegance but what the collective actually achieves: can agents coordinate without communication, evaluate without ground truth, and reason without shared state? This direction studies coordination and reasoning under the strictest informational constraints.

Overview

Most multi-agent systems assume some shared infrastructure: a communication channel, a common world model, or a ground-truth signal for evaluation. This direction removes those assumptions one by one. The result is a set of research questions that are both practically important and theoretically sharp: how does meaningful coordination emerge when agents can only observe outcomes? How do model ensembles evaluate their own outputs without gold labels? How do agents reason collaboratively when they cannot pool knowledge directly?

The unifying insight is that the hardest informational constraints force the most interesting solutions, and that those solutions tend to generalize to the messy real-world settings where shared infrastructure is unavailable or unsafe.

Zero-Knowledge Coordination

Decentralized task allocation under complete informational silence: no communication, no priors, no shared state. Coordination emerges from a public interaction broadcast and independent matrix-factorization policies.

Self-Evaluation

Ensemble-based evaluation where models rotate through teacher, student, and judge roles to build synthetic benchmarks and assess each other without external ground truth.

Collaborative Reasoning

Structured critique-and-improvement loops where agents iteratively challenge and refine each other's reasoning, with assessment and reporting built into the process.

Active Projects

ZKDroneSwarm Decentralized drone task allocation under zero-knowledge constraints with emergent coordination from interaction outcomes alone. CoEval Ensemble-based LLM evaluation framework where models rotate roles to build synthetic benchmarks without ground truth. CoReason AI-native learning platform for iterative reasoning practice through structured critique-and-improvement loops.
