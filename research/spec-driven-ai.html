Spec-Driven GenAI

Home / Research / Spec-Driven GenAI

The pragmatic test for generative AI is not output quality in the abstract but fitness to a stated purpose: does the molecule meet its detonation target, does the image match the steering preference, does the code satisfy its contract? This direction studies generation where the specification is a hard constraint, not a soft preference.

Overview

Generative AI is usually evaluated on quality: how realistic is the image, how fluent is the text? Spec-Driven GenAI shifts the question: how well does the output satisfy a measurable specification? The spec could be a physical property target for a molecule, a user preference trajectory for an image, a behavioral contract for generated code, or a constraint on tone and content for generated text.

The core challenge across all domains is the same: steer generation toward verifiable targets without collapsing output diversity or ignoring domain-specific structure. This requires rethinking how specifications are encoded, how they interact with the generative process, and how fitness is measured.

Molecular Generation

Domain-gated latent diffusion for novel energetic material discovery, where detonation velocity, density, and structural novelty are hard targets verified by DFT computation.

Image Steering

Interactive diffusion steering from text prompts into iterative preference-guided refinement loops, tracking user intent across multiple rounds.

Code and Text Generation

Speculative code generation under a forward-only commit protocol with structured gating, and constrained text generation that meets editorial and factual targets.
